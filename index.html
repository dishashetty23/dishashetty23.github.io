I came to analytics through engineering. My first years were spent at Mercedes-Benz R&D, analyzing sensor data for driver assistance systems and performing root cause analysis, where I learned that a good analysis can quite literally make vehicles safer. That experience taught me to treat data with rigor, because the stakes behind the numbers are real.

At PwC, I moved closer to the business side, building dashboards that surfaced key trends and driving automation initiatives for Fortune 500 clients across financial services, automotive, technology, and healthcare. Watching executives make decisions from visuals I built showed me how much clarity matters, and how rare it is.

Most recently, I completed my Master of Science in Business Analytics at the University of Washington, where my capstone combined machine learning and an AI-powered query agent to help a credit union keep its members. I love this field because every dataset hides a story, and finding it well is what turns numbers into action.
